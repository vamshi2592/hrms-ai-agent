# HR Policy Handbook

## Leave

The company offers three leave types: casual leave, sick leave, and earned
(privilege) leave. Casual and sick leave are credited at the start of the
calendar year. Earned leave accrues at 1.75 days per completed month of service
and can be carried forward up to 45 days.

Leave must be applied through the HRMS at least 2 working days in advance,
except sick leave which may be applied retrospectively with a medical
certificate for absences longer than 2 days.

Casual leave cannot be availed in December due to year-end financial close;
employees needing time off in December should use earned leave instead.

A maximum of 5 consecutive casual leave days can be taken at a time. Leave
during probation is limited to sick leave only.

## Payroll

Salaries are credited on the last working day of each month. Payslips are
available in the HRMS from the 1st of the following month. Investment proofs
for tax exemption must be submitted by 31 January each year. Reimbursement
claims must be raised within 60 days of the expense.

Employees can view only their own payslips. Compensation bands and headcount
budgets are confidential and accessible to HR administrators only.

## Onboarding

New hires must complete the onboarding checklist within 15 days of joining:
signing the employment contract, completing IT security training, setting up
payroll bank details, and meeting their assigned buddy. Managers are notified of
any pending onboarding tasks for their reports after day 15.

## Performance Reviews

Performance reviews run twice a year in half-yearly cycles (H1 and H2). Self
assessment opens 3 weeks before the review deadline, followed by manager review
and calibration. Ratings are: Below Expectations, Meets Expectations, Exceeds
Expectations, and Outstanding. Ratings are visible to the employee, their
manager, and HR only.

## Grievances and Harassment

Any grievance, harassment complaint, or POSH (Prevention of Sexual Harassment)
report is handled confidentially by a human HR business partner and, where
applicable, the Internal Committee. These matters are never handled by
automated systems. Reports can be raised any time and are acknowledged within
24 hours.

## Working Hours and Remote Work

Standard working hours are 9 hours including a 1-hour break. Employees may work
remotely up to 2 days a week with manager approval. Fully remote arrangements
require HR approval and are reviewed every 6 months.
